# Crew Event Slice — People, Places, and Unverified Truth

## Product question

Can the shift feel like a living event rather than four colored work buttons, without turning the game into frantic multi-clicking?

## New playable layer

The existing production chain remains readable:

1. capture
2. edit
3. delivery
4. client communication

Two support spaces now sit beside it:

- backstage, where schedules, VIP movement, and group-photo assumptions circulate
- media desk, where publishing requirements, delivery myths, and raw-file requests spread

A third visible crew member can investigate those spaces while the photographer and workflow assistant continue their own work.

## Specialist choice

The player selects one partner for the shift:

- stage documentary photographer: faster backstage checks and extra capture relief
- news photographer: faster media-desk checks and extra delivery relief
- people-first photographer: every resolved rumor also lowers client pressure

These are fields of practice, not character rarity or combat classes. The same rumor therefore creates different useful responses depending on who joined the team.

## Gossip rule

One piece of plausible team gossip appears during each case. Its wording offers context, but the UI does not mark the correct source. The player chooses whether the source is more likely backstage or at the media desk.

- a correct check converts uncertainty into operational relief
- a wrong check consumes crew time but leaves a second attempt possible
- an unchecked rumor hardens into pressure and deadline loss
- every result remains recoverable and is explained after delivery

The point is not trivia. It is recognizing that event work is shaped by unverified information moving between people.

## Causal upgrades

After the first two deliveries, the player turns one observation into a persistent SOP improvement:

- capture: dual-position coverage shortens capture work
- edit: a hot folder shortens ingest and edit work
- delivery: a prebuilt delivery structure shortens packaging and upload work
- client: a single-window record slows client-pressure growth

The upgrade is framed as one less future coordination step, not a score multiplier. It persists for the remaining shift and appears directly on the upgraded station.

## Current boundaries

- one specialist joins each shift; a full multi-photographer roster is not implemented yet
- support spaces react to rumors but do not yet host independent coverage assignments
- upgrades reset between shifts; long-term studio memory is not implemented yet
- there is no store, rarity, loot, equipment level, or blocking event dialog
