# First Real Shift — Equipment, Signals, and Absurd Reality

## Product question

Can the player prepare for uncertainty without discovering one universal loadout or memorized click order?

## Slice

Before the three-case shift, the player hears four incomplete field signals and packs two of four concrete tools:

- backup body
- dual card readers
- mobile hotspot
- paper runbook

The three cases draw distinct incidents. One signal is noise; the player is not told which one, which case an incident belongs to, or when it will arrive. Two equipment slots mean at least one exposed risk remains.

## Fairness rule

The incident can surprise the player. The consequence cannot feel arbitrary.

After delivery, the player must be able to trace:

1. what was visible beforehand
2. which equipment could have absorbed it
3. what time or pressure cost appeared without that equipment
4. why the case remained recoverable

## Current incidents

- the main camera fails only when the subject arrives
- the sole card reader falls to 12 KB/s
- venue Wi-Fi shows full bars but cannot reach the internet
- the VIP is at the door while every stakeholder remembers a different schedule

## Success criteria

- equipment reads as a real workflow affordance, not a stat bonus
- the player cannot cover every sign in one shift
- a contained incident feels like preparation quietly removing future work
- an exposed incident changes rhythm without demanding repeated tapping
- client pressure remains visible during the disruption
- the completion Field Note explains cause and consequence without grading the player
- a replay changes the incident order or combination enough to resist a fixed script

## Next depth, not yet implemented

- equipment weight, setup time, maintenance, and shared power/network constraints
- incidents caused by people, permissions, changing authority, and conflicting instructions
- SOPs learned from exposed incidents that reduce future coordination steps
- room objects and team habits that remember previous shifts
