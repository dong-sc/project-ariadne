# Sprint 2 Playtest — A Three-Case Shift

## Product question

Does changing the risk signature of each case make the player observe first, or do they still repeat one memorized gesture?

## Run

Play all three cases without reloading:

1. **活動快訊** — balanced baseline
2. **品牌肖像** — client communication becomes urgent earlier
3. **晚宴急件** — deadline and delivery pressure become the dominant read

The player should discover that a complete workflow is not only fast production. It also means sequencing one assistant between handoff preparation and client updates. The assistant can only do one parallel task at a time, so each brief should change which risk deserves attention first.

## Observe

- Before the first click, can the player describe what is different about the current brief?
- Does the player understand why the assistant cannot prepare a handoff and reply to the client simultaneously?
- Does the player prepare the handoff first on the rush case, but respond earlier on the portrait case?
- Does **交接備妥** reduce the need to keep checking the current station?
- After a clean case, is **有餘裕** felt as lower cognitive pressure rather than merely read as a label?
- Does clearing the third case feel like the end of a work period, not just another restart screen?

## Success criteria

- the three cases create visibly different first priorities
- assistant movement makes the current parallel task readable without opening a menu
- preparing a handoff lowers that station's pressure before the photographer arrives
- clean workflow lowers the next case's starting pressure and extends its deadline
- incomplete workflow still delivers, but the debrief explains what remained unresolved without punishment
- the third delivery creates a distinct, quieter shift-complete state
- the player wants to replay to produce a cleaner three-case run, not to increase a score
