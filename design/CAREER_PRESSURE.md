# Career Pressure — The Work Before the Work

## Product question

Can a five-to-ten-minute workday feel different before the first dispatch, while making a long career more frighteningly recognizable than a simple upgrade tree?

## Design references

This slice follows three useful constraints from outside the project:

- *Into the Breach* was designed around readability, limited menus, low numbers, short experiences, and interesting choices. Project Ariadne should become deeper by changing consequences, not by adding a large command surface. See the [GDC design postmortem](https://media.gdcvault.com/gdc2019/presentations/Into%20the%20Breach%20Postmortem%20Final.pdf).
- Lucas Pope described ambiguity as central to *Papers, Please*: the player often acts with little information and may not immediately see the result. Ariadne keeps that uncertainty, but its Field Notes must still make operational cause understandable afterward. See [T.M.I.: Preserving the suspension of disbelief through ambiguity](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/-t-m-i-preserving-the-suspension-of-disbelief-through-ambiguity).
- Scarcity research describes attentional tunneling: immediate pressure can redirect attention away from other information. Ariadne uses visible urgency to create that narrowing, then lets SOPs and delegation restore bandwidth. See [Scarcity and Inattention](https://bfi.uchicago.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/BFI_WP_2022-76.pdf).

## The new loop

Before equipment and specialist selection, one real-world dilemma changes the terms of the day:

1. an ambiguous request, favor, market comparison, body warning, credit dispute, or silent teammate appears
2. the player chooses how the cost will be carried
3. the choice changes the current workday's timing, pressure, handoffs, or information window
4. it also changes one persistent career condition
5. Shift Clear explains what the choice actually left behind

The player is not choosing good, neutral, or evil. The player is deciding where the debt lands.

## Persistent conditions

The interface avoids a spreadsheet and uses qualitative language:

- **stamina** — still has room / beginning to overdraw / the body is collecting
- **team trust** — proactively covers / follows instructions / the team goes silent
- **market standing** — can hold scope / repeated bargaining / price is anchored

These conditions have operational effects:

- low stamina slows production without creating an instant failure
- low trust makes handoff preparation slower; high trust helps both handoffs and field verification
- weak standing creates more opening client pressure; strong standing makes scope easier to hold

From Workday 5 onward, stamina periodically declines. This is not a punishment for playing longer. It forces the veteran fantasy to move from physical dominance toward judgment, process, trust, and delegation.

## Authored dilemmas

The initial pool includes:

- an undefined request for raw files
- a favor asking to insert untracked deliverables
- credit disappearing from the final report
- a younger photographer charging sixty percent while remaining faster and calmer
- a hand injury that will not reset overnight
- an assistant who has stopped anticipating the next step

Career tracks weight related dilemmas without guaranteeing them. A leverage-heavy career is more likely to meet team silence; a craft-heavy career is more likely to meet body debt; a network-heavy career is more likely to meet obligations.

## Replay structure

The first three workdays retain authored lineups. Later workdays remix one event from each risk profile, producing more combinations without breaking the readable capture / client / deadline archetypes.

Equipment, specialists, incidents, rumors, area SOPs, career tracks, career conditions, and the daily dilemma then cross over each other. Randomness selects the situation; authored rules explain the consequence.

## Boundaries

- no store, rarity, loot, equipment levels, or boss framing
- no blocking confirmation modal
- no tap-to-accelerate interaction
- no numeric health bar for a human career
- no hidden irreversible ending caused by a single random event
- changing careers remains a later authored outcome, not a failure screen
